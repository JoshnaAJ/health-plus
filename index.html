First Aid Guide

Principles and Practices of First Aid

Key Messages

  • First aid preserves life, prevents deterioration, and promotes recovery
  • It is a vital skill requiring training
  • Golden rules: Ensure safety first and perform tasks in a logical order

Golden Rules of First Aid

  1. Assess the situation for danger before acting
  2. Remove the victim from the cause of injury, if possible
  3. Resuscitate if necessary
  4. Loosen tight clothing
  5. Control bleeding and cover wounds
  6. Avoid unnecessary movement unless required
  7. Provide reassurance and seek help
  8. Improvise with available materials
  9. Treat for shock
  10. Transport the victim safely
  11. Stay with the victim until professional help arrives
  12. Keep emergency contacts handy for quick access

Assessment in First Aid

Key Steps

  1. Primary & Secondary Survey: Check airway, breathing, and circulation
  2. Diagnosis: Assess signs, symptoms, and history
  3. Scene Safety: Ensure a safe environment before providing care
  4. Assessing Responsiveness: Check for consciousness by gently shaking the person and calling out
  5. Check for Medical Identification: Look for medical bracelets or cards indicating pre-existing conditions

Structure and Function of Essential Body Parts

Key Points

  • Skull: Protects the brain
  • Spine: Houses the spinal cord; crucial in movement
  • Ribs & Breastbone: Protect the heart and lungs
  • Lungs: Assist in oxygen intake and carbon dioxide expulsion
  • Heart: Pumps oxygenated blood to the body
  • Abdomen: Contains vital organs such as the liver, kidneys, and intestines

Basic Life Support (Resuscitation)

Key Steps for CPR

  1. Check Responsiveness – Shake the person and call out
  2. Perform Chest Compressions – At least 2 inches deep at a rate of 100-120 per minute
  3. Give Rescue Breaths – Two breaths after every 30 compressions
  4. Repeat until Medical Help Arrives
  5. Use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) if available and follow voice instructions

Heimlich Maneuver (Choking First Aid)

  1. Stand behind the choking person
  2. Wrap arms around their waist
  3. Make a fist and place it above their navel
  4. Grasp the fist with your other hand and thrust sharply inward and upward
  5. Repeat until the object is expelled
  6. If the person is unconscious, begin CPR immediately
  7. Encourage small sips of water if the person is able to breathe but still feels discomfort

First Aid in Emergencies

Burns

    Signs & Symptoms:

    • Burns at contact points
    • Muscle pain and contractions
    • Difficulty breathing
    • Irregular heartbeat
    • Seizures or unconsciousness

Treatment Steps:

  1. Ensure scene is safe
  2. Turn off power source if possible
  3. Check breathing and pulse
  4. Begin CPR if necessary
  5. Cool any burns with clean water
  6. Seek immediate medical attention

Never touch the person while they are still in contact with the electrical source
