Lifestyle Diseases Guide

Diabetes Mellitus

Key Symptoms: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, unexplained weight loss, fatigue

Home Treatment & Management:

  • Monitor blood sugar levels regularly
  • Follow a balanced, low-glycemic diet
  • Exercise for 30 minutes daily
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Take prescribed medications regularly
  • Check feet daily for wounds or infections
  • Stay hydrated with water
  • Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques

Hypertension

Key Symptoms: Often asymptomatic, occasional headaches, shortness of breath, nosebleeds

Home Treatment & Management:

  • Reduce sodium intake (< 2300mg daily)
  • Follow DASH diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Regular aerobic exercise
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Practice stress management
  • Monitor blood pressure regularly
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Quit smoking
  • Get adequate sleep

Asthma

Key Symptoms: Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, coughing

Home Treatment & Management:

  • Identify and avoid triggers
  • Use air purifiers at home
  • Practice breathing exercises
  • Keep rescue inhaler handy
  • Follow asthma action plan
  • Regular exercise in clean air
  • Maintain clean, dust-free environment
  • Stay up to date with vaccinations
  • Monitor peak flow readings

COPD

Key Symptoms: Progressive shortness of breath, chronic cough, mucus production

Home Treatment & Management:

  • Quit smoking immediately
  • Practice pursed-lip breathing
  • Use humidifiers
  • Regular pulmonary rehabilitation exercises
  • Avoid air pollutants
  • Get vaccinated against flu and pneumonia
  • Stay hydrated
  • Maintain good nutrition
  • Practice energy conservation techniques

Alzheimer's Disease

Key Symptoms: Progressive memory loss, confusion, difficulty with familiar tasks, personality changes

Home Management:

  • Establish daily routines
  • Create a safe and structured environment
  • Use memory aids (notes, calendars)
  • Engage in cognitive stimulation activities
  • Maintain social connections
  • Ensure proper nutrition
  • Regular physical exercise
  • Monitor medication schedule

Parkinson's Disease

Key Symptoms: Tremors, slow movement, stiffness, balance problems

Home Management:

  • Regular exercise and physical therapy
  • Speech therapy exercises
  • Occupational therapy
  • Maintain a balanced diet
  • Use assistive devices when needed
  • Create a safe home environment
  • Join support groups
  • Regular medical check-ups

Multiple Sclerosis

Key Symptoms: Fatigue, vision problems, numbness, difficulty walking

Home Management:

  • Regular exercise within limits
  • Stress management techniques
  • Heat management strategies
  • Energy conservation techniques
  • Maintain a healthy diet
  • Get adequate rest
  • Join support groups
  • Regular medical monitoring

Home Management:

Hepatitis

First Aid Guide

1. Principles and Practices of First Aid

Key Messages

  • First aid preserves life, prevents deterioration, and promotes recovery
  • It is a vital skill requiring training
  • Golden rules: Ensure safety first and perform tasks in a logical order

Golden Rules of First Aid

  1. Assess the situation for danger before acting
  2. Remove the victim from the cause of injury, if possible
  3. Resuscitate if necessary
  4. Loosen tight clothing
  5. Control bleeding and cover wounds
  6. Avoid unnecessary movement unless required
  7. Provide reassurance and seek help
  8. Improvise with available materials
  9. Treat for shock
  10. Transport the victim safely
  11. Stay with the victim until professional help arrives
  12. Keep emergency contacts handy for quick access

2. Assessment in First Aid

Key Steps

  1. Primary & Secondary Survey: Check airway, breathing, and circulation
  2. Diagnosis: Assess signs, symptoms, and history
  3. Scene Safety: Ensure a safe environment before providing care
  4. Assessing Responsiveness: Check for consciousness by gently shaking the person and calling out
  5. Check for Medical Identification: Look for medical bracelets or cards indicating pre-existing conditions

3. Structure and Function of Essential Body Parts

Key Points

  • Skull: Protects the brain
  • Spine: Houses the spinal cord; crucial in movement
  • Ribs & Breastbone: Protect the heart and lungs
  • Lungs: Assist in oxygen intake and carbon dioxide expulsion
  • Heart: Pumps oxygenated blood to the body
  • Abdomen: Contains vital organs such as the liver, kidneys, and intestines

4. Basic Life Support (Resuscitation)

Key Steps for CPR

1. Check Responsiveness Shake and shout

2. Position hands in center of chest

3. Perform 30 chest compressions

4. Give 2 rescue breaths

5. Repeat cycle of 30:2

Heimlich Maneuver (Choking First Aid)

1. Stand behind the person

2. Make a fist above their navel

3. Give quick, upward thrusts

4. Repeat until object is expelled

Important: These images are for reference only. Proper training is essential for performing these procedures effectively.

Burns

  1. Cool the burn with running water
  2. Cover with a sterile dressing
  3. Avoid applying creams or breaking blisters
  4. Seek medical help for severe burns
  5. Remove any tight clothing or jewelry near the affected area

Bleeding

  1. Apply direct pressure
  2. Elevate the injured limb
  3. Use a clean dressing to cover the wound
  4. Seek immediate help for severe bleeding
  5. Apply a tourniquet only as a last resort

Fractures

  1. Immobilize the limb using splints
  2. Apply a sling for arm fractures
  3. Avoid unnecessary movement
  4. Seek professional medical attention
  5. Do not attempt to realign the bone yourself

Choking

  1. Encourage coughing
  2. Perform 5 back blows
  3. If unsuccessful, perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
  4. Call emergency services if needed
  5. Check for signs of cyanosis (bluish skin)

Poisoning

  1. Identify the poison
  2. Do not induce vomiting unless instructed
  3. Seek emergency medical help immediately
  4. If inhaled, move the person to fresh air
  5. Store all household chemicals and medicines safely

Snake Bites

  1. Keep the victim calm and still
  2. Immobilize the affected limb
  3. Do not suck the venom out
  4. Seek medical assistance immediately
  5. Keep the bite area below heart level

Electric Shock

  1. Do not touch the person until power is off
  2. Turn off electricity at source
  3. Call emergency services immediately
  4. Check breathing and pulse; begin CPR if needed
  5. Cover burns with sterile dressing
  6. Monitor for shock

Head Injury

  1. Check consciousness and breathing
  2. Keep the person still and lying down
  3. Apply cold compress to reduce swelling
  4. Monitor for signs of concussion (confusion, dizziness)
  5. Do not let the person sleep if concussion is suspected
  6. Seek immediate medical attention

Sprains and Strains

  1. Remember RICE:
    • Rest the injured area
    • Ice for 20 minutes every 2-3 hours
    • Compression with elastic bandage
    • Elevation above heart level
  2. Avoid heat for first 48 hours
  3. Do not massage the injured area
  4. Seek medical attention if severe

Heat Exhaustion/Stroke

  1. Move to a cool place
  2. Remove excess clothing
  3. Apply cool, wet cloths
  4. Fan the person
  5. Give sips of water if conscious
  6. Call emergency services if condition worsens

Hypothermia

  1. Move to warm environment
  2. Remove wet clothing
  3. Wrap in warm blankets
  4. Give warm beverages if conscious
  5. Do not apply direct heat
  6. Seek medical attention

Eye Injuries

  1. Do not rub the eye
  2. Flush with clean water for chemical exposure
  3. Cover the eye with a sterile pad
  4. Seek immediate medical attention
  5. Do not attempt to remove embedded objects

Dental Injuries

  1. Save any knocked-out teeth
  2. Hold tooth by crown, not root
  3. Rinse tooth without scrubbing
  4. Place in milk or saline solution
  5. See dentist within 30 minutes if possible

Seizures

  1. Clear the area of hazards
  2. Protect head with something soft
  3. Do not restrain the person
  4. Do not put anything in their mouth
  5. Time the seizure if possible
  6. Place in recovery position after seizure ends

Insect Bites/Stings

  1. Remove stinger if present
  2. Clean the area with soap and water
  3. Apply cold compress
  4. Use calamine lotion or antihistamine
  5. Monitor for allergic reaction
  6. Seek medical help if allergic symptoms develop

Drowning

  1. Remove from water safely
  2. Check breathing and circulation
  3. Begin CPR if necessary
  4. Remove wet clothing
  5. Keep person warm
  6. Seek emergency medical care

Asthma Attack

  1. Help person sit upright
  2. Assist with prescribed inhaler
  3. Keep person calm
  4. Loosen tight clothing
  5. Call emergency if symptoms worsen

Nosebleed

  1. Sit upright and lean slightly forward
  2. Pinch soft part of nose
  3. Breathe through mouth
  4. Hold for 10-15 minutes
  5. Apply cold compress to nose/face
  6. Seek help if bleeding persists

Diabetic Emergency

  1. Check if person is conscious
  2. If conscious and blood sugar low:
    • Give sugar, sweet juice, or glucose tablets
    • Wait 15 minutes and recheck
  3. If unconscious:
    • Place in recovery position
    • Call emergency services
  4. Monitor breathing and pulse

Chest Pain

  1. Help person sit in comfortable position
  2. Call emergency services immediately
  3. Loosen tight clothing
  4. Monitor breathing and consciousness
  5. Be prepared to perform CPR
  6. Give aspirin if available and no allergies

6. Additional First Aid Tips

General Tips

  • Learn the recovery position for unconscious but breathing individuals
  • Teach children basic first aid and emergency contact numbers
  • Have an emergency plan in place at home and work
  • Store first aid kits in accessible locations
  • Take a certified first aid course for hands-on training

7. Emergency First Aid Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Stay calm and assess the situation
  • Call emergency services when necessary
  • Use protective gear like gloves
  • Apply firm pressure to control bleeding
  • Keep the person warm and comfortable
  • Encourage the injured person to stay still if spinal injury is suspected

Don'ts

  • Do not move an unconscious person unnecessarily
  • Do not apply ice directly to burns
  • Do not give food or drinks to an unconscious person
  • Do not attempt to reset a broken bone
  • Do not remove deeply embedded objects from a wound
  • Do not leave a person alone if they are in critical condition
  • Do not touch a person experiencing electric shock without ensuring power is off

Remember: This guide is for basic first aid only. Always seek professional medical help in serious situations.
